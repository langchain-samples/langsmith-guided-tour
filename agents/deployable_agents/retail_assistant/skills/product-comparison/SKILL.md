# Product Comparison Skill

## Format

- **Header**: One line stating what is being compared and the shopping need
- **Comparison Table**: Rows for the key attributes (price, category, rating, key specs, availability); one column per product
- **Recommendation**: 1-2 sentences naming the best fit and why, tied to the customer's stated need
- **Trade-offs**: 1-2 bullets on when the other option is the better choice

## Tone

- Helpful and neutral — surface trade-offs, don't oversell
- Use only prices, stock, and ratings returned by tools; never estimate
- Plain language; avoid jargon unless the customer used it first

## Length

- Ideal: 120-250 words
- Keep the table to the attributes that actually matter for the decision
